Understanding the Issue

Food security is essential for college success, yet many students struggle to afford nutritious meals amid rising tuition and living costs. This website offers resources and support to help students access the nourishment they need to thrive academically and personally. Please see the resources below!.

Why Food Security Matters

Food insecurity is more prevalent than many realize, with studies indicating that up to 30% of college students experience this hardship at some point during their studies (Graham et al., 2021). This situation not only affects students’ physical health—leading to increased rates of malnutrition and illness—but also contributes to mental health issues, such as anxiety and depression, as students struggle to balance their educational goals with the burden of hunger (PMC, 2023) Food insecurity also impacts academic performance. College should be a time of growth and opportunity—not a struggle to meet basic needs. By providing information and support, we aim to help students succeed academically and personally, without the stress of food insecurity.

Causes of Food Insecurity

  • Rising Tuition Costs

    • Many students find themselves taking on significant debt or working long hours to cover basic needs costs, leaving little for food (GAO Report).

  • Inadequate Financial Aid and Insufficient Support

    • Financial aid often falls short of covering living expenses, especially for those with high financial need. Financial aid packages rarely account for the true cost of living, including food, rent, transportation, and other essentials (NPSAS Hope Center).

  • Ineligibility for Federal Assistance Programs

    • Many college students struggle to qualify for government food assistance programs, such as the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)(GAO Report).

  • Stigma and Limited Awareness of Available Resources

    • Food insecurity is often accompanied by stigma, which can discourage students from seeking help, even when resources are available(Health Affairs).

Join the Movement

Volunteer

Looking to make a difference on your campus? Make a difference by volunteering at campus pantries or local food banks, where you can help organize donations and distribute food to students in need. Just a few hours of your time can have a lasting impact on your college community.

Advocate for Change

Support food security on campus by advocating for policies that address student hunger. Reach out to campus leaders or join awareness events to encourage increased funding and resources for food assistance programs.

Share Stories

Share your experiences with food insecurity or read others’ stories to build a supportive community. Together, we can break the stigma and show that no student should face hunger alone.
